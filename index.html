Dr. Alexander Mendez, is a postdoctoral fellow at Johns Hopkins University (JHU) working on observational astrophysics using large multi-wavelength data-sets. His diverse interests include observing super massive black holes (SMBHs) at different wavelengths, placing galaxies within a cosmological context, and understanding the co-evolution of SMBHs and the galaxies where they reside. Prior to JHU, Dr. Mendez received his PhD in physics from the University of California, San Diego working with Professor Alison Coil, and he received his Batchelor of Science degrees in Mathematics, Physics and Astronomy from the University of Washington.
