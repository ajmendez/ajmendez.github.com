Alexander Mendez

Hi, I am Dr. Alexander Mendez, a postdoctoral fellow at The Johns Hopkins University (JHU) working in observational astrophysics using large multi-wavelength data-sets. I study the relationship between super massive black holes (SMBHs) at different wavelengths, place galaxies within a cosmological context, and disentangle the co-evolution of SMBHs and the galaxies in which they reside. Prior to JHU, I received my doctorate in physics from the University of California, San Diego working with Professor Alison Coil, and I received my Batchelor of Science degrees in Mathematics, Physics and Astronomy from the University of Washington. I enjoy coffee, code, and cycling.
