Downloadable Content for Astronomers

We have created a few versions for you to use in your astronomical presentations and talks. The figures are saved as Portable Network Graphics (PNG) and videos are encoded with ffmpeg as H264 mp4 movies. Both play under the latest versions of VLC, Apple Keynote, and Microsoft Powerpoint as of 01/2016.

We release them under xxx Licence, allowing you to present to public audiences. We ask if you to send a quick email to Alex if you decide to use any of the following visualizations in your talks.

SDSS "Cone Diagram" showing the distribution of spectroscopic galaxies and quasars in the SDSS North Galatic Cap footprint. Horizontally we show the Right Ascension angle, and vertically we show the redshift, scaled to linear lookback time. We show objects from a thin declination slice (20 < Dec < 30), to accentuate the large scale structure. Objects are shown with their optical color (g,r,i) mapped into (RGB) (Lupton et al. 2004). The abrupt changes in color at z~0.8, z~2.6 are due to changes in the targeted sample. We vary the size of each object for clarity of the presentation.

[Public Version] A downloadable copy of the above movie. We show spectroscopic stars, galaxies and quasars observed in the SDSS north galatic cap. In the upper panel we show a equal-area projection (Hammer-Aitoff) of the RA/Declination centered on a Declination 180. Objects are shown with their optical color (g,r,i) mapped into (RGB) (Lupton et al. 2004). In the lower panel we show binned average object color as the timeline.

[Astronomer Version] Same as the public version (center panel) without introduction clip, and subtitles. If you use this in your talk please leave me a quick message of where it is being presented. This version includes labeled axes and a detailed cone diagram for reference
